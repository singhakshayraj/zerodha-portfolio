📖 How to Use This Report

  1. Alpha Score is the model's prediction of how much this stock will outperform Nifty50 over the next 30 days. A score of +5% means the model expects the stock to beat Nifty by 5%.
  2. Focus on top-ranked stocks (Rank 1–15) that also have a healthy RSI (30–65). These have the best combination of momentum and room to run.
  3. RSI alone is not a buy/sell signal — use it to avoid chasing overbought stocks (RSI > 70) or catching falling knives (RSI < 30 without a reversal signal).
  4. Portfolio stocks marked ★ — if your holding is ranked low (> rank 50), consider whether to reduce position. If ranked high (< rank 20), it may be worth adding.
  5. Refresh weekly — run the script every Monday before market open for the freshest signals.

Alpha Score — What It Means

Strong Buy
Score ≥ +5% — Model strongly expects this stock to beat Nifty. High conviction signal. Consider adding or initiating position.
Outperform
Score +2% to +5% — Stock likely to beat market but with moderate confidence. Good candidate for entry or hold.
Neutral+
Score 0% to +2% — No strong edge either way. Hold existing position but don't add aggressively.
Underperform
Score -3% to 0% — Model expects underperformance vs Nifty. Review your thesis. Consider trailing stop.
Avoid
Score < -3% — Strong underperformance signal. If holding, consider reducing. Do not add.

RSI — Relative Strength Index

RSI measures how fast a stock has moved recently. It ranges 0–100. It tells you if a stock is overheated or oversold — not whether the company is good or bad.

RSI < 30
Oversold — Stock has fallen a lot recently. Often a good entry zone if fundamentals are intact. Market may have overreacted.
RSI 30–45
Weak but recovering — Stock is depressed but not extreme. Watch for reversal candles before entering.
RSI 45–65
Neutral zone — Ideal sweet spot. Stock has momentum without being overheated. Best risk/reward for new entries.
RSI 65–70
Strong momentum — Trending well. Okay to hold, but don't chase. Set a trailing stop loss.
RSI > 70
Overbought — Stock has run up fast. High risk of a pullback. Avoid fresh entries; consider booking partial profits.
